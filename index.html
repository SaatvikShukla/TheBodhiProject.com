THE FIRST PROJECT

Bringing to Life, the Walls of a Shelter Home

It all started on the 11th of September when Mr. Kushal Jadhav and Ashutosh Mishra--now the founder and co-founder of The Bodhi Project respectively--came up with the idea of giving out radiant strokes of happiness to the less fortunate.

This idea evolved into a need to bring about smiles on the faces on countless people, a need to help. They got in touch with Phalguni Bhaumik who is acquainted with a lot of NGOs in Pune. Phalguni introduced them to an orphanage of girls in Guruwar Peth, Pune and brought up the issue of the deteriorating walls which desperately needed fixing.

From this, Ashutosh Mishra then decided to improve the walls with graffiti, for which he created a strong team of students from his batch in Symbiosis Institute of Design. The Bodhis led by Pooja Jayan were successful in bringing to life the walls of St. Michael's Girls' Shelter Home. They managed to paint the walls in vivid themes in under 7 hours.

With a remarkably talented team ready, all that was needed next was sponsorship which was provided to them by Make A Difference through HDFC bank. The immense success of the endeavour encouraged The Bodhis, headed by Ashutosh under the watchful guidance of Mr. Kushal Jadhav, that the first seed planted needs to be BUILT TO BE STRONG AND EVERLASTING.

THE FIRST TEACHING PROGRAM

Initiation of Teaching Co-curricular Subjects

Our next step after the installation of the library at Saraswati Shiksha Ashram, Dapodi, was to do our part in helping the boys in a few co-curricular subjects.

We wanted to keep up the smooth collaboration we had initiated with SASA and look at our long term plans with the place. The children there did not have access to learning non-academic subjects like other children do in their school. It was then that the founder, Ashutosh Mishra and the Centre Head for the Ashram, (then Aaroh Dhoundiyal), with three volunteers set up a plan of teaching co-curricular subjects like Music, Art and Dance every Sunday. Besides that, they also set up a plan to teach spoken English to improve their language skills.

.

Since then, groups of three volunteers have been commuting to the Shiksha Ashram every Sunday to teach these subjects in a way that would not steal the free play hours of their precious weekend.
